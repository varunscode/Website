1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

IT is increasingly becoming the driving force for business around the world. Traditionally IT has been used within businesses for storing their customer and sales data but has evolved to solve a plethora of business needs. Nowadays IT is used for advanced analytics to identify trends that can be used to make informative decision for the business. With the implementation of machine learning and AI businesses are becoming more and more automated which reduces the need for human input. Nowadays entire businesses can be run from the comfort of your own home.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

Insufficient planning can cause a lot of digital projects to fail. Planning allows you to predict some of the issues that you may face as you execute the project and plan provisions accordingly. The reliability of your technology is also a large contributing factor to the success of digital projects. If your computer is to crash so will your progress on the project so to minimise this risk its essential to save work frequently and even upload it online so that a backup is available in case something goes wrong.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

GDPR stands for general data protection regulation and is a legislation that changes the way personal data is processed. When users input data into digital projects this data would be liable to GDPR and must be dealt with and kept safe according to the guidelines.

1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

For something to qualify for copyright it must be original and created by its author. If you recreate this content without permission from its owner, you are considered to be breaching this copyright data. It should be noted that copyright does not provide you with ownership of the material, it only prevents copying of it [2].
